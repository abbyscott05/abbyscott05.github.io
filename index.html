About Me

When I was in middle school, my Grandma gave me a simple robot called a Boe-Bot. My Grandma still typed up letters on a typewriter. How she was inspired to give me such a mechanical toy I will never know. But I quite enjoyed the present. I would write little programs that instructed the bot to follow a course of masking tape on my bedroom floor. It was all very exciting.

I am now a Junior at Stanford studying physics and computer science. My freshman year, myself and partner won the best project award for CS 107E when we built a 2D plotter drawing robot. I created a CAD model for the machine and laser cut it out of a wood composite. We wrote all the software and firmware bare metal on a Raspberry Pi.

My sophomore year, I began taking deep learning classes and other higher level CS classes. For a final project in a machine vision class, my partner and I retrained the final layers of a 3D convolutional neural network model to identify American sign language words. I then spun up a Flask backend and React frontend and built a simple website that allowed users to record a video of themselves signing a word and the model would output its top five guesses. That summer, I leveraged my work with deep learning for a physics fellowship where I worked to emulate the Rubin Observatory’s deblending pipeline with a RNN trained on simulated images.

For my professional background, I have now worked as a software developer at two different companies. In the winter and spring of 2021, I worked for StoneAge, a robotics waterblast company, doing full stack with Node.js/React and IoT device development with the AWS IoT core. That summer I worked for Boecore on a Missile Defense Agency project as a modeling and simulation intern developing applications in C++ and Java with an emphasis on Object Oriented Programming and Test-Driven Development. During the school year, I work as a tutor for both Schwab Learning Center at Stanford and Ivy Bound.

I also work with the Stanford Space Initiative satellites team. Currently I am working on a computer vision project with a Google Corral to classify the relative orientation of a cubesat from a picture taken by another cubesat. I wrote python script to generate training images using the 3D modeling software Blender.

In my free time, I enjoy exploring the mountains with friends, mountain biking, kayaking, playing ultimate, and reading.

Projects

Classical Machine Vision Satellite Orientation Algorithm

The goal was to identify the relative position and orientation of Satellite A based on a picture from Satellite B. In order to generate test data, I used the 3D modeling software Blender to create photorelistic images of a cubsate with Leds on the corners. I then wrote a classical machine vision algorithm using openCV to crop in on the Satellite and apply a PNP algorithm to identify the relative position and rotation.

2D plotter robot from scratch

Worked on this plotter robot as a final project for CS107E. All the software was written from scratch on a bare metal raspberry pi by myself and a partner. I personally did all of the mechanical design and fabrication.

Rubin Observaotry Deblending Emulator

I used PyTorch to develop different neural network architecture to emulate the Large Synoptic Survey Telescope (LSST) deblending pipeline in order to better understand systemic bias in weak lensing probes as a result of unrecognized galaxy-galaxy blends.
